Physics of a Rotating Boat

In this section we examine the boat rotation caused by its rudder deflection. We will try to derive the angular motion equations for the boat rotating under the effect of its rudder, that can provide a reasonable approximation.

Definitions

Consider a boat of length L rotating with an angular velocity ω due to a torque T applied by its deflected rudder. This rudder torque T will be resisted by a counter torque TR. We assume that the boat is rotating around a rotation axis passing through a point located at a distance x from the stern along the boat's longitudinal centerline (therefore x < L).

The resistance torque TR will be due to the water resistance as the body of the boat is rotating in the water (the corresponding air resistance is neglected). We split this resistance into 2 parts: TF and TA. The torque TF is the resistance torque caused by the water resistance of the front part of the boat and TA is caused by the water resistance of the aft (back) part of the boat.

Since we have split the resistance torque TR into its 2 components TF and TA it follows that:

TR = TF + TA

We wish to derive TR(k, ω), i.e. a formula of the resistance torque TR as a function of the the boat water resistance coefficient along its lateral axis k and the boat's angular velocity ω during rotation.

Separating TR in forward and aft components

The rotational motion of the boat can be separated into two parts: the front rotational motion ahead of the pivot point and the aft rotational motion before the pivot point. Modelling these two different components separately will allows us to calculate TF and TA independently.
